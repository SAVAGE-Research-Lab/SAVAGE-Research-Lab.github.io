Zhou Qian, PhD, master's supervisor, associate professor, and Communist Party member, is currently the director of the Institute of System Security and Reliability Engineering Applications at Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications. She completed her bachelor's and master's degrees in Communication Engineering and Information Security, respectively, at the National University of Defense Technology, and earned her doctorate in Computer Applications from Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics.  

Dr. Zhou long has been engaged in research on applied cryptographic algorithm design, network security and privacy, IoT technology, as well as fundamental theory and applications in databases. The course she teaches, "Network Security Technology (Bilingual)," won first prize in the Jiangsu Province Micro-Lecture Teaching Competition. Her research team, "Eclipse Savage Wings," won first place in the national finals of the 14th "China Software Cup" College Student Software Design Contest, and Dr. Zhou was honored as an "Outstanding Instructor."  

As the principal investigator, she has led various vertical projects such as the National Natural Science Youth Fund Project, open topics of national laboratories, and demonstrations for key national projects. She has also participated in multiple research initiatives, including the National High-Tech R&D Program (863), National Basic Research Program (973), and key national R&D programs. Leveraging these projects, she has published over 30 academic papers in high-level domestic and international journals in recent years and has been granted multiple patents and software copyrights.
